Biomaterials and Material Science Nanocellulose

I am interested in Nanocellulose! There are three types of Nanocellulose: cellulose nanocrystals (CNCs), cellulose nanofibers (CNFs), and amorphous nanocellulose. CNCs are strong and used in things like composites, films, and medicine. CNFs are flexible and have a large surface area, making them useful for coatings, medicine, and thickening liquids. Amorphous nanocellulose is more fluid and used in products like hydrogels, biodegradable packaging, and stabilizing mixtures.
I am most interested in CNF nanocellulose, and am working on creating a biodegradable nanocellulose packing peanut. The video shown above inspired me many years ago, and helped spark my interest, hope you enjoy!
